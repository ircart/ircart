     10scrambling to get work from various artists (not to mention finishing my

10 15 10  That blows my mind.                                                         
    10I founded Apathy when I was 15 years old. It was at a time when everyone    
10    in the art scene seemed to have their own thing. We were born out of the    
10    ashes of a couple of smaller groups. We had zero quality control, which     
10    was kind of the point. The elitism that existed in the art scene back       
10    then was difficult to get past sometimes, and we wanted Apathy to be a      
10    place where you didn't have to worry about that. We wanted it to be fun.    
10    Amazingly, it worked out pretty well.                                       
    10Eventually, we merged with another small group called THRuST (or            
10    "Thirty Hippos Running Up Stairs Together" if you prefer). That's when      
10    my buddy Marc (aka Trip) came aboard, and things really started to take     
10    off. Some big name artists signed on, and next thing we knew we were        
10    putting out some quality stuff. People were excited for our releases.       
    10Inevitably, a lot of us started to do silly things like graduate high       
10    school. It became harder and harder to coordinate things. I got busted      
10    for some telecommunications hijinks and wasn't allowed to use a modem.      
10    At one point, I was mailing my pack submissions to Trip on a floppy         
10    disk.                                                                       
    10We could read the writing on the wall, and after 14 packs we decided to     
10    call it quits. We negotiated a merger of most of the group into the ANSI    
10    juggernaut known as iCE. I faded out of the picture at that point, but      
10    Trip (and a lot of other ex-APA guys) went on to be the artistic heart of   
10    iCE for several years after.                                                
    10---                                                                         
    10Cut to 2013. I stumble my way onto Facebook and discover a group called     
10    "Ansi/Ascii Artists Worldwide". As it turns out, I'm not the only one       
10    with fond memories of the art scene. I run into the guys from Blocktronics  
10    and I start releasing ANSI again for the first time in over 15 years. I'm   
10    thrilled to run into artists I knew back then - including a number of ex-   
10    Apathy guys. One thing leads to another, and the next thing I know we're    
10    planning a reunion pack.                                                    
    10And this is where things get sad. One of the artists I'd reconnected with   
10    was Eric "Ewheat" Turevon. He was a founding member of Apathy, and one of   
10    the only artists to stick with us through our entire run. He helped run     
10    the group when I ran into my legal troubles and he brought a sly sense of   
10    humor into the mix that helped cement our legacy. I got to know him again   
10    in the summer of 2013 and found him to be that same funny, caring guy       
10    that I knew as a kid. He was enthusiastically on board for an Apathy        
10    reunion.                                                                    
    10Then I got the news in February, 2014 that Eric had died.                   
    10We were all gutted. It seemed unreal, after just finding him again.         
10    We talked it over and decided to pay tribute to Eric, as well as former     
10    Apathy artist Mr. Self Destruct (who sadly passed away several years        
10    ago), in the best way we could - by dedicating this release to them and     
10    creating some great textmode art in their honor. I'm also proud to include  
10    the very last, unreleased work from MSD.                                    
    10---
