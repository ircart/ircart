1 7i'm coming up on the one year mark of drawing ansi again.  during the past     
7 year i thought it'd be fun to do tributes to ansi groups i always enjoyed      
7 during my days in the ansi scene way back when.  i never really had much of    
7 a 'go-to' list of what groups i felt i should do.  the groups that would       
7 come to mind were groups of people who really made that paticular group seem   
7 more like a group of friends with the objective of having fun creating art     
7 and not with a robotic mission objective of 'MUST...CREATE...BEST...ART...     
7 BEEP BOOP BEEP...NO...TIME...FOR...FUN..."                                     
7 the interesting thing to this approach of casual creativity, the people in     
7 these groups really became more than a name on a member list.  striving to     
7 be the best can sometimes lead to a robotic sense of doing things.  it makes   
7 people less likely to 'try new things' and continue the tradition of caring    
7 more about 'bigger is better' which ultimately leads to burning out and        
7 fading away.  overindulgence of ego is high on the risk factor of killing      
7 creativity.  this can be applied to just about every form of artistic outlet.  
7 it's why underground music and independent films seem to have a smaller        
7 fanbase than mechanically produced productions of the mainstream, but at the   
7 same time that smaller fanbase has a bigger appreciation of what's going on.   
7 which brings me back to the groups i liked doing these tributes for.  it'd     
7 give me a reason to go back in time and scour over every single pack they      
7 put out.  the key players in these groups weren't great because of the group   
7 name they would put after their own name when putting out their artwork.       
7 both their individual attitudes as artists and collective attitude of a        
7 group of people made the group more than just a bunch of people trying to      
7 'be the best' and make all other groups shake in their boots.  but in the end  
7 THEY started to become a powerhouse built from the integrity of artistic       
7 freedoms, and all out fun with fellow members.  so a few months ago when       
7 i saw misfit talking about the days of the ansi afro's, my sometimes slow      
7 brain went "click! yeah!  an apathy pic.  do one of those!"  (actually it      
7 went "cliiiiiick.....do...draw...ansi...apathy...afro ansi..." [because        
7 sometimes brain slow.])                                                        
7 i do believe a general consensus would say that apathy truly represented       
7 every single example of what a really great group should be from start to      
7 finish.                                                                        
7 upon finishing this drawing, i heard the news about one of their members       
7 and really just a delightful ruckus bringer to the entire scene, ewheat,       
7 passing away.  although, all these years later, i'm sure those closest to      
7 him during his days in the scene and later wouldn't regard him as a 'member,'  
7 but as a true friend and artistic brother.                                     
7 out of respect for ewheat and those who knew him as more than an artist, i     
7 can't truly express the signifigance of his loss on a personal level.  but     
7 as a fan of his artistic and creative energy, and someone who always enjoyed   
7 the enthusiasm of the people who talked about being a part of something with   
7 him, it does fill my heart with sadness.  i do hope that even if it's the      
7 slightest bit of positive light to break through, ewheat will always have      
7 a bit of himself around through those who knew him well and people like me     
7 who enjoyed hearing his artistic voice through his artwork.
